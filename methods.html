Pipeline

Methods

From a hexagonal sampling grid over Jakarta to two parallel computer-vision tracks — one estimating pedestrian gender presence, one assessing streetscape quality — feeding three neighbourhood-level indices.

Sampling strategy

A 50-metre hexagonal grid was overlaid across DKI Jakarta, excluding the Thousand Islands. Candidate hexagons were selected by stratified random sampling, weighted by distance to the nearest RPTRA park — ensuring the sample doesn't systematically over- or under-represent areas near public green space.

For each selected hexagon, the Google Street View Static API was queried to confirm imagery availability from 2013–2019 within a 25-metre radius of the hexagon centroid. Roughly half of the hexagons returned no imagery at all. Where imagery existed, one panorama per year was kept — the one nearest the centroid, when more than one was available — giving an average of 3.5 panoramas per covered hexagon.

Each retained panorama yields six images at headings 0°, 60°, 120°, 180°, 240°, and 300° (640×640px, full 360° coverage). Night images (luminance thresholding), interior shots (API panorama-type metadata), and duplicate frames (perceptual hashing) are removed before any model sees the data. Every retained image is assigned to its administrative kelurahan using Badan Informasi Geospasial boundary shapefiles.

Pipeline architecture

Two tracks run on the same image set. The pedestrian track is three stages — detect, gate, classify — producing the inputs to GPI. The segmentation track runs independently on the same panoramas to produce SQI.

Stage 0

Detection

Every crop that could be a pedestrian, found before it's judged. Runs inline during download rather than as a second pass.

  • YOLOv11L, COCO-pretrained
  • Confidence threshold 0.25
  • IoU > 0.3 excludes vehicle occupants
  • IoU > 0.1 excludes bike/motorbike riders
code_to_download_GSV_images.py

Stage 1

Rider gate

Jakarta's pedestrian counts are heavily confounded by motorbike riders unless filtered before classification.

  • EfficientNet-B0, 3-class softmax
  • Classes: rider / clean / hard-negative
  • Bounding box expanded 15% before crop
  • Only "clean" passes to Stage 2
photo_scanner_BIG.ipynb

Stage 2

Classification

An ensemble of two independently-trained models on the surviving crops, domain-adapted to Jakarta street imagery.

  • EfficientNet-B3 + YOLOv11M ensemble
  • Classes: adult female / adult male / child
  • "Unknown" is a post-hoc score-based flag, not a trained class
Feeds GPI directly
An earlier project summary described this as a two-stage system with a single 4-class classifier. The gate step (Stage 1) and the ensemble structure of Stage 2 were left out of that description — this page reflects the pipeline as actually deployed.

Streetscape segmentation

The segmentation track runs independently of the pedestrian track, on the same panoramas. A YOLOv8x-seg model trained on Mapillary Vistas — chosen for its geographic diversity, including tropical and informal urban settings the more common benchmark datasets lack — produces per-image pixel ratios across roughly two dozen streetscape categories: sidewalk, curb, crosswalk, road, lane marking, building, wall, fence, street furniture, vegetation, terrain, and more.

For the Streetscape Quality Index specifically, this rich output is distilled down to two components: a vegetation ratio and a ground-surface condition score, remapped from the full Mapillary category set. All segmentation inference runs directly on GSV imagery with no additional Jakarta-specific fine-tuning.

Spatial indices

Three indices are computed per kelurahan from the two tracks above.

GPI · Gender Presence Index

GPI = 0.35·FPR + 0.35·FCR + 0.15·(1−HHI) + 0.15·CPR
  • FPRFemale Presence Rate — share of images with a female detection
  • FCRFemale Count Ratio — female share of all non-unknown detections
  • HHIHerfindahl–Hirschman concentration of presence across time-of-day & weekday/weekend; (1−HHI) rewards habitual rather than occasional use
  • CPRChild Presence Rate — a proxy for caregiver safety judgement

Weights estimated via PCA against field-observed female pedestrian share at 48 validation sites. Field validation: r = 0.73 (p < 0.001).

SQI · Streetscape Quality Index

SQI = 0.5·VR + 0.5·PQS
  • VRVegetation Ratio — mean vegetation pixel share
  • PQSPavement Quality Score — share of ground-surface pixels in good condition

Validated against 36 expert street audits (Irvine–Minnesota Inventory protocol): r = 0.69 (p < 0.001).

SUI · Spatial Uncertainty Index

SUI = U / (F + M + C + U)
  • F, M, C, UCounts of female, male, child, and unknown detections in a kelurahan

Rather than discarding low-confidence detections, SUI turns them into an honesty check: kelurahan with SUI > 0.25 are flagged in every downstream economic analysis, so results aren't presented with more confidence than the underlying classifier actually has.
